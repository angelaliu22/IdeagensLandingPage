What is IdeaGens?

People often turn to the crowd to get fresh ideas for creative problems. But they often get mostly bad, generic, or duplicate ideas, and have to waste valuable time and effort iterating with the crowd several times before getting useful ideas. IdeaGens is designed to produce better ideas faster by allowing the requester to guide and inspire the crowd using a real-time facilitation interface.

How It Works
IdeaGens is a social ideation system for guided crowd brainstorming. It decomposes the complexity of brainstorming creative solutions into smaller, more manageable tasks and roles in order to facilitate the generation and identification of the best ideas. Specifically, ideators produce ideas while facilitators guide the brainstorm and synthesize the results at the end of the session.

Ideators product potential solutions for your problems. Crowd workers use IdeaGens' ideator interface to input new ideas for a given prompt.

Facilitators guide the crowd to explore specific areas of interest. As the facilitator, you can send inspirations to ideators that influence what they focus on when coming up with ideas.

Synthesizers distill the pool of ideas and extract the best ideas using the synthesis interface.When the brainstorm is over, synthesizers organize and evaluate the ideas in order to identify the most compelling solutions for your problems.

Driven by Research
IdeaGens was created a team of researchers and developers at Carnegie Mellon University’s ProtoLab, a research group situated in the Human-Computer Interaction Institute. Here at the ProtoLab, we conduct research focus at the intersection of social computing and design, with the goal of developing technologies that will solve complex problems in a creative an collaborative way. Our work is driven by research. Indeed, IdeaGens is informed by insights from hundreds of articles on topics such as creativity, ideation, group dynamics, data visualization, computer-supported cooperative work, and many more. If you’re interested in learning more about our work, feel free to visit the ProtoLab website to see what we’re working on!
